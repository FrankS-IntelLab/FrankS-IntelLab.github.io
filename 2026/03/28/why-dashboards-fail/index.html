Why Most Dashboards Fail

A dashboard that shows everything tells you nothing.

The Pattern

Teams build dashboards to feel in control. They add every metric they track. The result: a wall of numbers nobody reads.

What Actually Works

One primary metric per audience. An executive needs a different view than an engineer. Build for the decision, not the data.

Anomaly-first design. Don’t show me green numbers. Show me what changed and why it matters.

Actionable or nothing. If a metric can’t trigger a decision, cut it.

The Rule

Before adding any chart, ask: what action does this enable? If you can’t answer in one sentence, don’t add it.
